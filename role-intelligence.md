# Role Intelligence - Chief Operating Officer, AI Adoption

## Nominal role
Chief Operating Officer - AI Adoption & Consulting Firm.

## Actual work
Turn founder-led business development into a predictable, profitable, client-retentive professional-services operating system. Own client delivery, sales operations, human-and-agent capacity, staffing, business cadence, margin, cash flow, and the founder/COO working agreement.

## Central contradiction
The firm must preserve bespoke judgment and measurable client outcomes while creating enough repeatability to forecast, price, staff, deliver, renew, and improve.

## Operating tensions
- founder velocity vs operating independence;
- bespoke client work vs repeatable delivery economics;
- AI-agent leverage vs accountable human ownership;
- sales urgency vs scope and margin integrity;
- bench readiness vs utilization and cash discipline;
- async autonomy vs visible operating cadence;
- fast internal automation vs dependable evaluation and escalation.

## Stakeholders
Founder/CEO; client executive sponsors; client workflow owners and users; sales team; engagement leaders; consultants/builders; agent owners; finance; recruiting/people operations; external recruiting partner.

## Decision architecture
The COO must decide what can be sold, what needs delivery review, what is in scope, what staffing/agent mix can carry the work, when risk escalates, what evidence proves success, what renews, what changes pricing, and what no longer requires founder attention.

## First-year measures
Retention; client outcome acceptance; schedule and scope integrity; utilization and realization; margin and cash; pipeline-to-capacity accuracy; forecast-to-actual variance; escalation quality; onboarding speed; founder attention released; agent reliability, cost and improvement.
