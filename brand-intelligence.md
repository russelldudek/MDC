## Logo decision

The LinkedIn page exposes an official company-image URL, but the image bytes were not retrievable through the available build environment. The campaign therefore uses a restrained typographic nominative identifier rather than tracing, recreating or fabricating the official mark. This is a documented asset-availability exception, not a claim that the typed treatment is an official logo.

## Color and typography

Because the confidential employer is undisclosed and no authoritative client brand system is available, all color tokens are explicitly candidate-original. The palette uses warm paper, charcoal ink, copper, muted moss and alert red to support the architectural load-path metaphor. Typography uses a licensed/system serif-sans editorial pairing; no proprietary font files are copied.

## Candidate-facing integrity

The campaign does not identify or imply an undisclosed client. It does not claim employer sponsorship or approval. It labels all company-specific operating ideas as hypotheses for discovery.
